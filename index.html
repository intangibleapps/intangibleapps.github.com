Intangible Apps

Building apps that make life more fun, since 2010

What We're All About

We want to bring the experience of modern, designer board games to new audiences. We believe that modern mobile devices minimize the barrier-to-entry for board games by eliminating physical storage constraints, tedious setup and cleanup, and the need to always have a partner to play with.

Our Team

We are a team of 3 talented software developers with over 12 years of combined professional experience. The software we've written is used by hundreds of thousands of users. Our team consists of Mike Dellanoce, Brad Fitzwater, and Brian Hazzard.

Mike Dellanoce
Mike Dellanoce taught himself to program at age 16 by writing games with C++ and DirectX. Since graduating from Virginia Tech's computer science program, Mike has been developing large scale web applications for Rackspace where he has established himself as a technical leader and all-around programming savant. Outside of work Mike tinkers with the hottest new technologies, contributes to open source projects, and writes on his blog. Mike met Brad and Brian at Rackspace and discovered a common passion for mobile game development.
Brad Fitzwater
Brad Fitzwater has been writing software since he was 17. After six years at Marshall University, Brad earned two Bachelors' degrees (Business and Computer Science), with a minor in Mathematics. During his senior year, Brad was awarded Marshall University's "Computer Science student of the Year" and was one of 15 science and engineering students selected to present original research at the STaR Symposium, sponsored by NASA. Since graduation, Brad has worked on Content Management Systems and Geographic Information Systems. At Rackspace, Brad worked on a large scale web application, where he met Mike and Brian.
Brian Hazzard
Brian Hazzard is passionate about two things: software, and board games. After graduating Magna Cum Laude from James Madison University with a degree in Business, he pursued a career in the software industry. First, he developed tax software at Fast Enterprises that state governments depend on for collecting revenue. Next, he worked as a Technical Product Manager on a web-app that Rackspace customers rely on for business-critical hosted IT infrastructure. Now, he is combining his passions to create high-quality board game apps that gamers and their friends and family can rely on for an enjoyable way to connect with each other. His interest in board games was sparked by the iOS versions of Carcassonne, Medici, and Ra, so it is fitting that Brian will make his mark by creating brilliant board games on a brilliant platform.
